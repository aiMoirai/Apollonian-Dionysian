THE MUSEUM

Explore the rooms and discover the artworks

Beneath the whispers of the Oracle, the Temple of Apollo at Delphi exhumes its secrets to host a clash that defies time. The artworks lie hidden within the sacred core, unfolding through the shadows of the pronaos, the mystery of the cella, and the silence of the opisthodomos. Here, the icy order of the Apollonian and the wild ecstasy of the Dionysian stand face-to-face in an eternal duel, confronting each other from opposite sides of the ancient halls. As these rival souls challenge one another in the gloom, the very stones seem to vibrate with the pulse of the gods.

The journey is yours to command: click on the rooms to unveil their ancestral secrets and touch the artworks to trigger revelations and claim your deeper knowledge of this divine struggle.

Pronaos

The Pronaos was the entrance hall of the temple, a porticoed space preceding the main cella. Here visitors could pause before accessing the most sacred part of the temple. This area was often decorated with inscriptions and votive offerings, and represented the transitional point between the external world and the sacred space of the temple.

Cella

The Cella was the heart of the temple, the most sacred space where the statue of the deity was kept. Only priests could access it to perform religious rites. In this space, the most important ceremonies took place and the temple's treasures were stored, including precious offerings and ritual objects.

Opisthodomos

The Opisthodomos was the rear hall of the temple, symmetrical to the pronaos. This area was used to store sacred objects and temple treasures. Unlike the pronaos, the opisthodomos was not accessible to the public and represented the most reserved area of the temple complex.
