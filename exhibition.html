Exhibition

This exhibition brings together the complete collection of works that explore the Apollonian and the Dionysian as symbolic forces. Through artworks from different times and disciplines, it traces the dialogue between form and excess, rational control and emotional intensity, revealing how these principles continue to shape artistic expression.

Apollo of the Belvedere

A classical representation of Apollo emphasizing harmony, proportion, and idealized form.

Plato’s Phaedrus (Papyrus Fragment)

A manuscript witness to structured discourse, rhetoric, and the discipline of the soul.

Tragic mask

A theatrical object tied to classical drama and formalized representation.

De Divina Proportione by Luca Pacioli

A Renaissance treatise linking mathematical proportion to aesthetic harmony.

Apollo and the Muses

Apollo as a principle of measure and artistic order, surrounded by the Muses.

The Well-Tempered Clavier by Johann Sebastian Bach

A manuscript copy reflecting musical order through a systematic sequence of preludes.

Apollo and Daphne by Gian Lorenzo Bernini

A mythological scene where Daphne's transformation into laurel gives rise to Apollo's enduring symbol.

The Ideal City

A vision of rational space: perspective, symmetry, and ordered architecture.

Kylix with Apollo and kithara

Apollo as god of music: harmony and ritual order on an Attic drinking cup.

Bacchus and Satyr by Michelangelo

A sculpture where classical form meets intoxication, imbalance, and sensual excess.

The Triumph of Bacchus and Ariadne by Lorenzo il Magnifico

A carnival text celebrating pleasure and vitality while acknowledging transience.

Plato’s Symposium (Papyrus 843)

A papyrus witness to the dialogue of eros, banquets, and transformative desire.

Comic mask

A theatrical object tied to disguise, inversion, and collective release.

Euripides' Bacchae Fragments

Fragmentary papyri connected to Euripides and the educational afterlife of tragedy.

Bacchus by Caravaggio

A painting that foregrounds sensuality, intoxication, and fragile pleasure.

Sketches for the prelude and act one of Tristan und Isolde by Richard Wagner

Preparatory materials tied to desire, dissolution, and ecstatic intensity in music drama.

The City Rises by Umberto Boccioni

A vision of modern energy and collective force in constant upheaval.

Eye-cup with Dionysus

A symposiastic object where wine, myth, and transformation merge.
